Greek Ribs

Greek ribs are a mouthwatering and flavorful dish that draws inspiration from the rich culinary traditions of Greece. These succulent ribs are typically marinated in a blend of Greek herbs and spices such as oregano, garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and a touch of honey for a hint of sweetness. The marinade infuses the meat with a delightful combination of tangy, zesty, and aromatic flavors. The ribs are then cooked to perfection, whether grilled or roasted, resulting in tender, juicy meat with a slightly crispy exterior. This Greek twist on ribs offers a tantalizing taste experience that showcases the vibrant and robust flavors of the Mediterranean, making it a delightful choice for any meat lover or fan of Greek cuisine.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

Servings

6 servings

Tags: Dinner

Instructions

Step 1

In a mixing bowl, combine the olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, chopped oregano, chopped rosemary, honey, salt, and black pepper. Whisk or stir well to create the marinade.

Step 2

Place the pork ribs in a baking dish or container and pour the marinade over them, making sure the ribs are evenly coated. You can use your hands or tongs to rub the marinade into the meat. Cover the dish or container and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to infuse the meat.

Step 3

If grilling the ribs, preheat your grill to medium heat. If roasting, preheat your oven to 325째F (163째C).

Step 4

Remove the ribs from the marinade and let any excess marinade drip off. Reserve the marinade for basting or glazing, if desired.

Step 5

If grilling, place the ribs on the grill and cook for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, turning occasionally and basting with the reserved marinade. The ribs are done when they are tender and the internal temperature reaches at least 145째F (63째C).

Step 6

If roasting, place the ribs on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil. Roast in the preheated oven for approximately 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the ribs are tender and cooked through. Baste with the reserved marinade occasionally during the cooking process.

Step 7

Once the ribs are cooked, remove them from the grill or oven and let them rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute.

Step 8

Slice the ribs into individual servings, following the bone lines.

Step 9

Serve the Greek ribs on a platter, garnished with lemon wedges and fresh oregano or parsley. You can also serve them with tzatziki sauce for dipping or alongside a Greek salad or roasted vegetables.

Ingredients

Pork Ribs: Choose baby back ribs or spare ribs, depending on your preference and availability. Aim for about 2-3 pounds of ribs.

1/4 cup olive oil

3 tablespoons lemon juice

3 cloves garlic, minced

2 tablespoons fresh oregano, chopped (or 2 teaspoons dried oregano)

1 tablespoon fresh rosemary, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried rosemary)

1 tablespoon honey

1 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon black pepper
