Carne Asada

Carne Asada is a delicious Mexican dish featuring grilled, marinated beef. The meat is tender, juicy, and bursting with flavor thanks to a zesty marinade typically made with citrus juices, garlic, and spices. It is often served sliced and can be enjoyed in tacos, burritos, or as a standalone dish with rice and beans. Carne Asada is a favorite at gatherings and celebrations, offering a satisfying and vibrant taste of Mexican cuisine.

prep time

30 min.

cook time

15 min.

Servings

6 servings

Tags: Beef Dinner

Instructions

Step 1

Begin by preparing the marinade. In a mixing bowl, combine the lime juice, orange juice, minced garlic, soy sauce, olive oil, chopped cilantro, cumin, chili powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Whisk or stir well to ensure all the ingredients are thoroughly combined.

Step 2

Place the beef in a container or sealable plastic bag and pour the marinade over the meat, making sure it is evenly coated. Cover the container or seal the bag and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or ideally overnight, to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat and tenderize it.

Step 3

Once the meat has finished marinating, preheat your grill to medium-high heat. Make sure the grill grates are clean and lightly oiled to prevent sticking.

Step 4

Remove the meat from the marinade, allowing any excess marinade to drip off. Discard the remaining marinade.

Step 5

Grill the meat over direct heat for about 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the meat and your desired level of doneness. Flip the meat only once using tongs, and avoid pressing down on the meat to retain its juiciness. Adjust the cooking time as needed to achieve your preferred level of doneness.

Step 6

Once the meat is cooked to your liking, transfer it to a cutting board and let it rest for a few minutes to allow the juices to redistribute. Tenting the meat with aluminum foil during this resting period can help retain heat.

Step 7

Slice the Carne Asada against the grain into thin strips. This helps ensure tenderness and easy chewing.

Step 8

Serve the Carne Asada on tortillas as tacos or alongside rice and beans as a main dish. You can garnish with your choice of toppings such as salsa, guacamole, chopped onions, cilantro, and lime wedges.

Ingredients

Beef: Skirt steak or flank steak are traditional choices, but you can also use sirloin or ribeye. Aim for about 1.5 to 2 pounds of meat./p>

1/4 cup fresh lime juice

1/4 cup fresh orange juice

4 cloves garlic, minced

1/4 cup soy sauce

1/4 cup olive oil

2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro

1 teaspoon ground cumin

1 teaspoon chili powder

1 teaspoon paprika

1 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon black peppe

Tools

Grill

Tongs

Mixing Bowl
